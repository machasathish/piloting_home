  • M-1 or F-1 student visa
  • Valid passport
  • TSA approval

FAQs for Indian Students Considering Flight Training in the USA

Thinking about pursuing your pilot dream in the USA? It's a fantastic path, but it comes with specific requirements and considerations for Indian students. Here are some of the most common and crucial questions you should be asking:

Visa & Entry Requirements

For flight training/vocational, you'll primarily need an M-1 student visa. The process involves:
  • Getting an I-20 form from a SEVP-approved U.S. flight school
  • Paying the SEVIS fee
  • Completing the DS-160 online application
  • Attending an interview at the U.S. Embassy/Consulate in India

SEVP (Student and Exchange Visitor Program) approval means the U.S. government authorizes a school to enroll international students. Only SEVP-approved flight schools can issue the I-20 form required for your M-1 visa. Choosing a non-SEVP approved school will lead to visa denial.

Yes, absolutely. All non-U.S. citizens must obtain TSA clearance before beginning flight training in powered aircraft. This is a separate online application and fingerprinting process that must be approved before your first lesson.

No. The M-1 visa specifically does NOT permit any form of employment, whether on-campus, off-campus, or freelance. You must demonstrate sufficient funds to cover your entire training and living expenses without working.

The M-1 visa is generally valid for the duration of your training program as listed on your I-20, plus a 30-day grace period for departure. If training is delayed due to weather, aircraft maintenance, or other valid reasons, your school's DSO (Designated School Official) can help you apply for a program extension to maintain your legal status.
